Every town here clears the one non-negotiable: California's Lanterman entitlement covers all of them, so Maddy's services are guaranteed statewide — the difference is only which regional center holds her file, and a move between them is a transfer, not a restart.

So this page reads them on what actually differs. Housing is measured the way it matters to you — a 3bd/2ba rental under $3,000/month, and whether that band really exists there — not sale prices. Wildfire and the Berkeley transit commute are listed alongside, as reference, for you to weight however you want.

Grass Valley / Nevada City

A1 · SCOUTED
Nevada · Alta California Regional Center · 3/2 ~$2,200–2,700 · wildfire elevated

The scouted finalist — river, trails, gold-rush downtowns, BriarPatch co-op, and 3/2 rentals sitting comfortably under $3K. The two honest marks against it: real wildfire exposure, and no transit path to Berkeley. See the full scouted page →

~4 hr+
No rail in the county. Route 5 bus to Auburn (~50 min, weekdays only) then Capitol Corridor (~2 h 40, ~2 Auburn trains/day). In practice, ~2.5 hr by car — a driving relationship, not a transit one.

Martinez

A2 · RESEARCHED
Contra Costa · Regional Center of the East Bay · 3/2 ~$3,000–3,700 · wildfire low–moderate

Historic waterfront downtown, a marina, John Muir's home, and an enormous East Bay Regional Park network at the doorstep (Briones, Carquinez Strait) for Cooper. The honest gaps: 3/2 rentals mostly land at or above $3K, so your band is tight here; there's no true co-op (Bay Area grocers are ~20 min out); and the water is Delta and bay — for trout you're driving to the Sierra.

Capitol Corridor, Martinez → Berkeley, direct, ~14 trains/day. Walk or short bus to the platform, one train.

Fairfield / Suisun City

A3 · RESEARCHED
Solano · North Bay Regional Center · 3/2 ~$2,400–3,000 · wildfire low–moderate

The one new town where the rent band actually holds — 3/2 houses under $3K are available, with Suisun City the softer end. Suisun City's waterfront district is walkable and genuinely charming; Rockville Hills Regional Park covers dog trails. The trade: it's the weakest of the five on the clean-eating co-op and the walk-everywhere vibe — Fairfield proper is car-oriented, and the LGBTQ presence is suburban rather than a scene.

Benicia

A4 · RESEARCHED
Solano · North Bay Regional Center · 3/2 ~$3,000–3,700 · wildfire low

A compact former state capital on the Carquinez Strait with one of the most walkable Main Streets in the North Bay, an arts district in the old Arsenal, waterfront everywhere, and Benicia State Recreation Area shoreline for the dog. Low wildfire. The catch for you: 3/2 rentals sit right at $3K and up, so the band is tight — and the express bus to BART runs only about every two hours.

Rent tight (~$3K+)Very walkableLow fireBus every ~2 hrs
Commute to Berkeley · transit
~1 hr
SolTrans express bus (Benicia → Walnut Creek BART) then BART into Berkeley — about an hour all in, but the bus runs ~every 2 hours.

Davis

A5 · RESEARCHED
Yolo · Alta California — same RC as A1 · 3/2 ~$3,000–3,250 · wildfire low, flood moderate

The strongest everyday fit of the four new towns, and smoothest for Maddy: Davis sits in the same Alta California Regional Center as Grass Valley, so her file wouldn't even change hands. A true food co-op, Putah Creek's tailwater trout minutes away, and one of the most bike- and dog-friendly, LGBTQ-affirming towns anywhere. The catch: student demand keeps 3/2 rents around $3,000–3,250, so your band is tight, and summers run hot.

How to read this

On the corrected housing test, only Grass Valley/Nevada City and Fairfield/Suisun keep 3/2 rentals reliably under $3,000. Martinez, Benicia, and Davis can be done in that band, but you'd be hunting the bottom of their market. Wildfire and the Berkeley commute sit alongside as reference — weight them however fits the plan, rather than letting either drive the ranking.

Five towns, one gate cleared by all of them, and now the numbers that actually matter — rent first. The list stays honest; the weighting stays yours.
